Regulatory Compliance

Committed to the Highest Standards

At Biolyse Pharma Corp., regulatory compliance is not simply an obligation — it is a foundational principle that guides every stage of our operations, from laboratory development through to commercial manufacturing.

We operate under the oversight of Health Canada and adhere strictly to Canadian pharmaceutical regulations. Our compliance framework ensures that every product we manufacture meets rigorous safety, efficacy, and quality standards before it reaches patients and healthcare providers.

Good Manufacturing Practices (GMP)

Meeting and Exceeding cGMP Standards at Every Stage

Health Canada
Division 2, Part C — Food and Drug Regulations
Governs the manufacturing, packaging, labelling, and testing of drugs sold in Canada, including sterile injectables.
Health Canada Guidance
GUI-0001 — Good Manufacturing Practices Guidelines
The primary GMP guideline for pharmaceutical manufacturers in Canada, aligned with ICH Q7 and PIC/S standards.
ICH Harmonization
ICH Q7 — Active Pharmaceutical Ingredients
International guidelines for GMP of APIs, adopted by Health Canada and applied to Biolyse's manufacturing processes.

Current Good Manufacturing Practices

Biolyse operates cGMP-compliant manufacturing facilities designed to meet the standards set out under the Food and Drug Regulations (Canada) and Health Canada's GMP Guidelines (GUI-0001). Our facilities are subject to inspection by Health Canada's Health Products and Food Branch Inspectorate.

cGMP compliance governs all aspects of our production environment — from equipment qualification and environmental monitoring to personnel training and documentation control.

  • Facility design and environmental controls validated to cGMP standards
  • Equipment qualification (IQ, OQ, PQ) and ongoing calibration programs
  • Cleanroom manufacturing environments for sterile injectable production
  • Batch record documentation and full traceability for every production run
  • Mandatory personnel training and competency verification programs
  • Change control systems governing any modifications to processes or materials

Drug Approval & Licensing

Licensed, Reviewed, and Authorized by Health Canada

Health Canada
Establishment Licence (EL)
Required for all Canadian pharmaceutical manufacturers under Section C.01A of the Food and Drug Regulations. Authorizes Biolyse to fabricate, package, label, and test drug products.
Health Canada
Drug Identification Number (DIN)
Each drug product marketed in Canada must hold a DIN issued by Health Canada following a review of safety, efficacy, and quality data.
Regulatory Pathway
New Drug Submission (NDS) / Abbreviated NDS
Biolyse supports both originator and generic drug development pathways, navigating the applicable Health Canada submission process for each product.

Health Canada Authorization

All drug products manufactured by Biolyse Pharma Corp. are subject to Health Canada's drug authorization framework under the Food and Drugs Act. No product proceeds to market without the appropriate regulatory authorization in place.

Biolyse holds a valid Establishment Licence issued by Health Canada, which authorizes our facility to conduct pharmaceutical manufacturing activities. This licence is subject to ongoing compliance and periodic renewal.

  • Valid Establishment Licence covering fabrication, packaging, labelling, and testing
  • DIN authorization for each marketed drug product
  • Support for NDS and Abbreviated NDS (ANDS) submissions for new and generic products
  • Post-market change reporting and supplement submissions as required
  • Compliance with Health Canada's labelling and packaging regulations

Committed to Ethical Employment and Responsible Supply Chains

Ethical Labour Practices

Biolyse Pharma Corp. is committed to conducting business in a manner that respects human rights and promotes ethical labour practices throughout our operations and supply chain. We do not tolerate forced labour, child labour, human trafficking, or any form of exploitation.

Our workforce is employed voluntarily, compensated in accordance with applicable laws, and provided with a safe and respectful working environment. We expect our suppliers and service providers to uphold similar standards and to comply with all applicable labour and employment regulations.

  • Zero tolerance for forced, bonded, involuntary, or trafficked labour
  • Prohibition of child labour in all company operations
  • Compliance with applicable employment, labour, and human rights legislation
  • Expectation that suppliers maintain ethical labour practices
  • Commitment to responsible sourcing and supply chain oversight

Quality Assurance Systems

A Pharmaceutical Quality System Built on ICH Q10 Principles

📋

Standard Operating Procedures

All manufacturing and laboratory operations are governed by controlled SOPs. Documents are version-controlled, reviewed on a defined schedule, and accessible only through our document management system.

🔬

Analytical Testing & Release

Products undergo rigorous in-process and final release testing against approved specifications. Test methods are validated in accordance with ICH Q2(R1) guidelines before use in release or stability testing.

📦

Supplier Qualification

Raw material suppliers are assessed and approved through a formal qualification process. Materials are tested upon receipt and released only after meeting defined acceptance criteria.

🔄

Deviation & CAPA Management

All deviations from established procedures are documented, investigated, and resolved through a formal Corrective and Preventive Action (CAPA) system to prevent recurrence.

📊

Stability Programs

Ongoing and real-time stability studies are conducted under ICH Q1A(R2) conditions to support shelf-life assignments and detect any product degradation trends over time.

🗂️

Internal Audit Program

Scheduled internal audits assess compliance across all departments. Findings are tracked to closure and reviewed by senior management as part of the annual product quality review process.

Environmental & Workplace Safety

Responsible Operations Under Ontario and Federal Regulations

Ontario Regulation
Occupational Health and Safety Act (OHSA)
Governs workplace health and safety obligations for all Ontario employers, including hazard identification, worker training, and safe handling of pharmaceutical compounds.
Federal
Canadian Environmental Protection Act (CEPA)
Biolyse manages waste streams and chemical handling in compliance with CEPA and applicable provincial environmental regulations.
Transport Canada / Health Canada
Transportation of Dangerous Goods (TDG)
Applicable to the transport of pharmaceutical compounds classified as controlled or hazardous goods under the TDG Regulations.

Safety and Environmental Responsibility

Biolyse Pharma Corp. operates in compliance with Ontario's Occupational Health and Safety Act and all applicable federal and provincial environmental regulations. We recognize that responsible pharmaceutical manufacturing extends beyond product quality to include the safety of our people and our community.

Pharmaceutical manufacturing involves potent compounds, solvents, and controlled substances that require careful handling and disposal. Our environmental and safety programs are designed to minimize risk at every stage.

  • Hazard identification and risk assessments for all manufacturing processes
  • Controlled substance handling in compliance with the Controlled Drugs and Substances Act (CDSA)
  • Pharmaceutical waste disposal through licensed third-party waste contractors
  • Workplace safety training for all staff including WHMIS 2015 (GHS) compliance
  • Environmental monitoring and spill prevention programs
  • Joint Health and Safety Committee (JHSC) operating as required under OHSA
